\sect{1. Yes, the universe is a simulation}
OPH uses ``simulation'' in the hard mathematical sense. A physical world is an
admissible record-update structure whose observer patches can read one another,
repair disagreement, and return to the same structure after readback. The
symbol \(\mathfrak U_{\rm OPH}\) denotes the selected OPH universe. The map
\(T\) is the observer-overlap repair map [O1,O2]. The notation
\(\operatorname{Fix}(T)\) means the states returned unchanged by that map:
\[
  T(\mathfrak U_{\rm OPH})=\mathfrak U_{\rm OPH}.
\]
Read the equation as the simulation claim stripped to its mechanism. The
universe is the state that survives its own observer-overlap audit. Finite
records enter, overlap repair runs, contradictory branches die, and the
returned object is the same object.

\sect{2. Two constants from closure}

\sect{3. Gravity without units}
The gravity proof is short because \(P_\star\) cancels. OPH first derives the
Newton coupling as a geometric conversion between cell area and shared-edge
entropy [O3,O4]:
\[
  G_{\rm geom}
  =
  \frac{a_{\rm cell}}{4\bar\ell_{\rm shared}} .
\]
Here \(G_{\rm geom}\) is Newton's coupling in OPH geometric units,
\(a_{\rm cell}\) is the area of one observer cell, and
\(\bar\ell_{\rm shared}\) is the shared edge-entropy weight of that cell. The
equation makes gravity the conversion factor between an area count and the
edge entropy an observer patch shares with its neighbors. The factor \(4\) is
the quarter-area normalization from horizon entropy and the OPH edge-entropy
branch.

The selected pixel gives
\[
  a_{\rm cell}=P_\star\ell_\star^2,
  \qquad
  \bar\ell_{\rm shared}=\frac{P_\star}{4}.
\]
The symbol \(\ell_\star\) is the OPH length quantum, so
\(\ell_\star^2\) is the OPH area quantum. The first equation assigns physical
area to one cell; the second assigns the same cell its shared entropy weight.
Both use \(P_\star\), because \(P_\star\) is exactly the dimensionless pixel
ratio.

Substitution gives the dimensionless gravity result:
\[
  \frac{G_{\rm geom}}{\ell_\star^2}
  =
  \frac{a_{\rm cell}/\ell_\star^2}{4\bar\ell_{\rm shared}}
  =
  \frac{P_\star}{4(P_\star/4)}
  =
  1 .
\]
The pixel branch fixes every symbol in this line. Divide the geometric Newton
coupling by the OPH area quantum and the ratio is one. The same \(P_\star\)
measures cell area and shared edge entropy, so the pixel value cancels instead
of becoming an adjustable gravitational constant.

Thus
\[
  G_{\rm geom}=\ell_\star^2 .
\]
The local gravity theorem is one line. The numerical row is unit conversion.

\sect{4. Gravity as a number in SI units}
SI units enter through the clock bridge [O4], with exact SI constants [S3,S4].
OPH writes the bridge as
\[
  \gamma_\star
  =
  \frac{\ell_\star\nu_{\rm Cs}}{c}
  =
  \frac{\varepsilon_{\rm Cs}}{2\pi}.
\]
The symbol \(\gamma_\star\) is the dimensionless ratio between the OPH length
\(\ell_\star\) and the distance light travels during one cesium-clock tick.
The symbol \(\nu_{\rm Cs}=9\,192\,631\,770~{\rm s^{-1}}\) is the exact SI
cesium frequency, \(c\) is the exact speed of light, and
\(\varepsilon_{\rm Cs}\) is the OPH cesium clock gap emitted by the atomic
clock branch. This expresses the OPH length quantum in human clock units. The
\(2\pi\) appears because the clock branch uses angular phase while SI
frequency counts cycles.

Solving for the length quantum gives
\[
  \ell_\star
  =
  \frac{c\,\varepsilon_{\rm Cs}}{2\pi\nu_{\rm Cs}} .
\]
This turns a dimensionless clock gap into a length. Light travels
\(c/\nu_{\rm Cs}\) meters per cesium cycle, and the OPH phase gap supplies the
fraction of that cycle.

The geometric-to-SI conversion is
\[
  G_{\rm SI}
  =
  \frac{c^3}{\hbar}G_{\rm geom}.
\]
Here \(G_{\rm SI}\) is Newton's constant in
\({\rm m^3\,kg^{-1}\,s^{-2}}\), and \(\hbar\) is the reduced Planck constant.
The equation converts the OPH area coupling into the SI Newton coupling. The
usual Planck-area relation is \(\ell_P^2=\hbar G/c^3\), so a geometric area
coupling becomes an SI gravitational coupling after multiplication by
\(c^3/\hbar\).

Combining the last two equations with \(G_{\rm geom}=\ell_\star^2\) gives
\[
  G_{\rm SI}
  =
  \frac{c^5}{4\pi^2\hbar\nu_{\rm Cs}^2}
  \varepsilon_{\rm Cs}^2 .
\]
This computes the SI Newton constant from the OPH clock gap and exact SI unit
definitions. The powers are just bookkeeping: \(\ell_\star^2\) contributes
\(c^2\varepsilon_{\rm Cs}^2/(4\pi^2\nu_{\rm Cs}^2)\), and the area-to-SI
conversion contributes \(c^3/\hbar\).

The selected clock branch emits the clock gap [O4]:
\[

  3.1139305134160128239\ldots\times10^{-33},
  \qquad
  \gamma_\star
  =
  4.9559743365484194637\ldots\times10^{-34}.
\]
\(\varepsilon_{\rm Cs}\) is the branch output. \(\gamma_\star\) is the same
output in the length chart.

Substitution gives
\[
  G_{\rm OPH}

\]
