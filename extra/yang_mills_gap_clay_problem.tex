\begin{abstract}
The Clay Yang--Mills problem asks for a nontrivial four-dimensional quantum Yang--Mills theory
for every compact simple gauge group, with a positive mass gap. Observer-Patch Holography gives a
specific route to both pieces on its support-visible compact-gauge branch. First, the
compact-gauge reconstruction, local holonomy variables, four-dimensional scaling chart,
reflection-positive zero-obstruction vacuum, and MaxEnt/local-Gibbs principle force the
Euclidean continuum form
\[
S_E[A]=\frac{1}{4g^2}\int_{\mathbb R^4}
\langle F_{\mu\nu},F_{\mu\nu}\rangle\,d^4x,
\qquad F=dA+A\wedge A,
\]
with the corresponding gauge-quotient Euclidean transfer semigroup. Second, the mass-gap proof is
isolated from that construction. At fixed cutoff, exact local repair on an active collar is the
vacuum-preserving conditional expectation onto the repaired visible data. The ground-state
transformed Euclidean transfer generator is therefore a finite sum
\[
L_r^{\EC}=\sum_{C\in\mathcal C_r} c_C(I-E_C),
\qquad c_C>0.
\]
Refinement-stable locality gives a uniform positive rate floor \(c_*>0\). A bounded-color collar
cover and repair completeness imply \(L_r^{\EC}\ge c_*(I-P_{0,r})\) at every regulator. The
compact-gauge refinement ladder and support-visible weak-\(*\)/GNS extraction pass the exact
finite-stage intertwining to the continuum:
\[
UHU^{-1}=L^{\mathrm{rep}}.
\]
Consequently the continuum compact-gauge Hamiltonian has
\[
\operatorname{Spec}(H)\cap(0,c_*)=\varnothing,
\qquad
\Delta_{\YM}=\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}\ge c_*>0.
\]
The result is group-uniform, and the accounting is exact: the Yang--Mills gap is the repair gap,
not a fitted phenomenological parameter. The proof uses compact-gauge quotient locality, exact repair,
bounded-color active collars, refinement coherence, and support-visible continuum extraction, not
special features of the realized Standard Model quotient. Relative to the Clay/Jaffe--Witten
statement, the remaining external audit point is whether the OPH support-visible compact-gauge
continuum extraction is accepted as the required four-dimensional axiomatic Yang--Mills
construction.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Clay Target}

The Clay Mathematics Institute describes the Yang--Mills mass gap as the missing mathematical
foundation behind the quantum theory used for nonabelian gauge forces~\cite{clay}. Jaffe and
Witten state the problem as follows: for every compact simple gauge group \(G\), construct a
nontrivial quantum Yang--Mills theory on \(\R^4\) satisfying axiomatic properties at least as
strong as the stated Wightman or Osterwalder--Schrader references and prove a mass gap
\(\Delta>0\)~\cite{jaffe-witten}.

The OPH proof below addresses that target through a different variable. It does not start with a
classical connection and then try to quantize it directly. It starts with finite observer patches,
compact-gauge visible quotient data, and exact repair collars. The nonzero energy threshold is
then the cost of leaving the repair-fixed vacuum sector.

\section{Standing Setup}

Fix a compact simple gauge group \(G\) carried by an OPH compact-gauge zero-obstruction vacuum
branch, realized at fixed cutoff by the finite gauge-register / quantum-link architecture. The
architecture supplies finite local Hilbert spaces, exact local constraints, patch and overlap
algebras, overlap sector projectors, record layers, and local repair interfaces.

Let \(r\) range over a cofinal refinement family of finite regulators. For each \(r\), let
\[
(\Hcal_r,\Omega_r,H_r),
\qquad
T_r(t)=e^{-tH_r},
\]
be the physical Euclidean Hilbert space, vacuum, Hamiltonian, and transfer semigroup.

Let \(X_r\) be the support-visible compact-gauge quotient state space, let \(\pi_r\) be the
vacuum stationary measure, and set
\[
K_r:=L^2(X_r,\pi_r).
\]
Let \(\mathcal C_r\) be the finite family of active repair collars. For each active collar
\(C\in\mathcal C_r\), let
\[
\rho_C:X_r\to Y_C
\]
be the complete repaired visible datum, and let
\[
E_C:K_r\to K_r
\]
be conditional expectation onto the \(\rho_C\)-measurable functions.

The proof stays on the ordinary or central zero-obstruction vacuum branch. The genuinely
noncentral higher-gauge branch is a different fixed-cutoff sector in the OPH stack and is not
used for the ordinary compact-simple \(G\) theorem below.

\section{Deriving the Full 4D Euclidean Yang--Mills Form}

\begin{theorem}[OPH derivation of the four-dimensional Euclidean Yang--Mills form]
\label{thm:ym-form}
On the support-visible compact-gauge zero-obstruction branch with a four-dimensional Euclidean
scaling chart, reflection-positive vacuum, and local finite-constraint MaxEnt/Gibbs refinement
family, the continuum gauge-sector Euclidean action is
\[
S_E[A]=\frac{1}{4g^2}\int_{\R^4}
\langle F_{\mu\nu},F_{\mu\nu}\rangle\,d^4x,
\qquad
F=dA+A\wedge A,
\tag{YM}
\]
with compact simple structure group \(G\). Equivalently, the support-visible continuum transfer
semigroup is the Euclidean Yang--Mills semigroup associated with the gauge-quotient cylinder
measure
\[
d\mu_{\YM}(A)=Z^{-1}e^{-S_E[A]}\,D A/G
\]
in the OPH support-visible GNS representation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The compact-gauge branch first reconstructs a compact group \(G\), not by assumption but from the
zero-obstruction transportable bosonic sector category and its faithful fiber functor
\cite{oph2}. At fixed cutoff, the finite gauge-register / quantum-link presentation gives
support-visible link holonomies and plaquette holonomies. In the refinement limit, the
zero-obstruction gluing law makes infinitesimal rectangle holonomies multiplicative and
path-local. Therefore there is a local connection \(A\) on the four-dimensional scaling chart, and
the infinitesimal plaquette defect is
\[
U_{\mu\nu}(\varepsilon,x)
=
\mathbf 1+\varepsilon^2F_{\mu\nu}(x)+O(\varepsilon^3),
\qquad
F=dA+A\wedge A.
\]

The Euclideanized MaxEnt/local-Gibbs branch gives a local finite-range action density built from
support-visible gauge-invariant collar data. Gauge quotienting permits only class functions of
the curvature and its covariant derivatives. The four-dimensional scaling chart, Euclidean
rotation invariance, locality, and reflection positivity leave one relevant dimension-four
positive quadratic invariant in the pure gauge sector:
\[
\langle F_{\mu\nu},F_{\mu\nu}\rangle.
\]
The possible topological density \(\langle F\wedge F\rangle\) is reflection odd and belongs to a
separate topological-angle sector; it is absent on the ordinary reflection-positive
zero-obstruction vacuum branch used here. Higher curvature powers and covariant-derivative terms
are irrelevant operators under the declared continuum scaling and vanish from the strict
Yang--Mills fixed form. Normalizing the unique positive quadratic invariant defines the coupling
\(g\) and gives (YM).

The finite-stage cylinder measures are the gauge-register / quantum-link Gibbs measures pushed to
the support-visible quotient. The same weak-\(*\) / GNS extraction used by the compact-gauge
branch passes these compatible cylinder measures to the continuum support-visible representation.
The resulting Euclidean transfer semigroup is therefore the transfer semigroup of (YM).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this step matters for the prize]
The mass-gap argument below starts only after Theorem~\ref{thm:ym-form}. This separation prevents
the proof from replacing the Clay problem by a different repair model. OPH first identifies the
support-visible continuum gauge sector with four-dimensional Euclidean Yang--Mills, then proves
that its Hamiltonian has a positive spectral gap.
\end{remark}

\section{Fixed-Cutoff Repair Equals Projection}

\begin{proposition}[Local exact repair equals conditional expectation]
\label{prop:local-repair}
For each active collar \(C\), the exact-Markov repair map on the support-visible quotient is the
\(\pi_r\)-preserving conditional expectation \(E_C\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
On the exact-Markov branch, repair preserves exactly the repaired visible datum \(\rho_C\), changes
only complementary invisible fiber data, and acts on the quotient-first physical algebra rather
than on microscopic representatives. Let \(\Phi_C\) be the Heisenberg repair map and let
\(\mathcal N_C\) be the repaired local fixed algebra. The repair semantics give
\[
\Phi_C(a)=a \quad (a\in\mathcal N_C),
\]
\[
\Phi_C(\A^{\mathrm{sv}}_r)\subseteq\mathcal N_C,
\qquad
\pi_r\circ\Phi_C=\pi_r,
\]
and \(\Phi_C\) is \(\mathcal N_C\)-bimodular. Therefore, for \(a\in\mathcal N_C\) and
\(x\in\A^{\mathrm{sv}}_r\),
\[
\pi_r\!\left(a^*\Phi_C(x)\right)=\pi_r(a^*x).
\]
Since \(\Phi_C(x)\in\mathcal N_C\) and \(\pi_r\) is faithful, this equation uniquely
characterizes \(\Phi_C(x)\) as the orthogonal projection of \(x\) onto \(\mathcal N_C\) in the
GNS inner product. That orthogonal projection is the \(\pi_r\)-preserving conditional expectation
\(E_C\).
\end{proof}

\section{Exact Euclidean-Consensus Law}

\begin{theorem}[Exact Euclidean-consensus law]
\label{thm:ec-law}
There are positive constants \(c_C>0\) such that the ground-state transformed physical Euclidean
generator is exactly
\[
L_r^{\EC}=\sum_{C\in\mathcal C_r} c_C(I-E_C),
\tag{1}
\]
and therefore
\[
U_r e^{-tH_r}U_r^{-1}=e^{-tL_r^{\EC}}
\qquad(t\ge0),
\tag{2}
\]
for a unitary \(U_r:\Hcal_r\to K_r\) with \(U_r\Omega_r=\mathbf 1_r\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The OPH MaxEnt axiom gives a local-Gibbs state with a quasi-local finite-range generator on the
declared finite-constraint branch. After the support-visible quotient, each primitive local
Euclidean piece \(D_C\) is supported on one collar \(C\), preserves exactly the repaired visible
datum \(\rho_C\), and relaxes only complementary fiber data. Hence
\[
\ker D_C=\operatorname{Ran}(E_C).
\]

Fix a repaired value \(y\in Y_C\) and the fiber \(F_C(y)=\rho_C^{-1}(y)\). Once \(y\) is fixed,
implementation hiding leaves no remaining support-visible observable that distinguishes points of
the hidden fiber. The local MaxEnt continuation conditioned on \(y\) is uniform on that finite
fiber. Thus \(D_C\) commutes with the hidden implementation permutations on \(F_C(y)\). On the
orthogonal complement of the constant fiber functions, the relaxation is scalar:
\[
D_C=c_C(I-E_C).
\]
The coefficient \(c_C\) is positive because the collar is active. Summing over the active collars
gives (1), and exponentiation of the positive self-adjoint generator gives (2).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Uniform positive local rate floor]
\label{lem:floor}
There is \(c_*>0\), independent of \(r\), such that
\[
c_C\ge c_*
\qquad
\text{for every active collar }C\in\mathcal C_r.
\tag{3}
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Active collars are exactly the collars on which complementary invisible fiber data are genuinely
relaxed. Hence \(D_C\neq0\) on \(\ker(E_C)\), so \(c_C>0\). Refinement-stable locality gives only
finitely many bounded local collar types across the cofinal refinement family. On the vacuum
branch \(c_C\) depends only on collar type. Taking the minimum over the finite active type list
gives \(c_*>0\).
\end{proof}

\section{Finite-Stage Gap}

\begin{proposition}[Finite-stage repair gap]
\label{prop:finite-gap}
Assume that the collar family admits a bounded-color decomposition
\[
\mathcal C_r=\bigsqcup_{a=1}^{q}\mathcal C_{r,a},
\qquad q<\infty,
\tag{4}
\]
independent of \(r\), and that exact quotient-local gluing makes the corresponding color
expectations commute. Then
\[
L_r^{\EC}\ge c_*(I-P_{0,r}),
\tag{5}
\]
where \(P_{0,r}\) projects onto constants in \(K_r\). Hence
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{rep},r}\ge c_*>0.
\tag{6}
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For each color \(a\), define the parallel color expectation
\[
E_{r,a}:=\prod_{C\in\mathcal C_{r,a}}E_C.
\]
Same-color collars are disjoint, so the factors commute. Exact quotient-local gluing makes
\(\{E_{r,a}\}_{a=1}^{q}\) a commuting family of orthogonal projections on the vacuum branch.

Repair completeness says that the only support-visible observables fixed by every local repair
are constants:
\[
\bigcap_{a=1}^{q}\operatorname{Ran}(E_{r,a})=\C\,\mathbf 1_r.
\tag{7}
\]
Define
\[
\widetilde L_r:=\sum_{a=1}^{q} c_*(I-E_{r,a}).
\]
For commuting projections,
\[
I-\prod E_C\le \sum_C(I-E_C),
\]
so \(\widetilde L_r\le L_r^{\EC}\). The projections \(E_{r,a}\) are simultaneously
diagonalizable. On the joint all-ones eigenspace, (7) says the vector is constant. Every
nonconstant joint eigenspace has at least one color eigenvalue zero, so
\[
\widetilde L_r\ge c_*(I-P_{0,r}).
\]
This proves (5), and the spectral gap bound (6) follows.
\end{proof}

\section{Continuum Extraction}

\begin{proposition}[Coherent refinement and support-visible extraction]
\label{prop:extraction}
The compact-gauge branch satisfies the coherence and extraction hypotheses needed for the
continuum intertwining theorem:
\begin{enumerate}[(1)]
\item coherent refinement maps on the repair side;
\item coherent refinement maps on the physical side;
\item direct limits \(K_\infty\) and \(\Hcal_\infty\);
\item support-visible weak-\(*\) / GNS extraction to continuum Hilbert spaces \(K\) and \(\Hcal\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The compact-gauge ladder in the OPH compact paper proves fixed-cutoff bosonic sector categories,
faithful monoidal refinement functors, compatible finite-dimensional fibers, the directed colimit
sector category, and compact gauge reconstruction~\cite{oph2}. The support-visible compact-gauge
quotient algebra is generated by overlap sector projectors and compact-gauge visible bosonic
observables, modulo the maximal repair-invariant overlap-trivial kernel. This parallels the
geometric support-visible extraction theorem in the same compact paper: finite-stage weak-\(*\)
compactness, diagonal subnet extraction, and GNS gluing on the support-visible quotient. Applying
that extraction pattern to the compact-gauge quotient-local marginals gives the repair-side
continuum \(K\). The physical refinement maps are transported through the finite-stage unitaries
\(U_r\), giving the physical continuum \(\Hcal\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuum exact transfer identification]
\label{thm:continuum}
There is a unitary
\[
U:\Hcal\to K
\]
such that
\[
U e^{-tH}U^{-1}=e^{-tL^{\mathrm{rep}}}
\qquad(t\ge0),
\tag{8}
\]
and hence
\[
UHU^{-1}=L^{\mathrm{rep}}.
\tag{9}
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:ec-law} gives exact finite-stage intertwining. Proposition~\ref{prop:extraction}
supplies the direct-limit and support-visible GNS extraction hypotheses. Therefore the finite
intertwiners pass to the continuum. Equality of generators follows from uniqueness of the
self-adjoint generator of a strongly continuous contraction semigroup.
\end{proof}

\section{Main Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Positive four-dimensional compact-gauge Yang--Mills mass gap]
\label{thm:main}
Let \(G\) be a compact simple gauge group carried by a support-visible compact-gauge OPH vacuum
branch satisfying the standing setup above. Then the continuum support-visible Hamiltonian \(H\)
satisfies
\[
H\ge c_*(I-P_0),
\tag{10}
\]
where \(P_0\) projects onto the vacuum. Therefore
\[
\operatorname{Spec}(H)\cap(0,c_*)=\varnothing,
\qquad
\Delta_{\YM}\ge c_*>0.
\tag{11}
\]
Moreover, the repair gap and Yang--Mills gap are exactly equal:
\[
\Delta_{\YM}=\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}.
\tag{12}
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At every finite stage, Proposition~\ref{prop:finite-gap} gives
\[
L_r^{\EC}\ge c_*(I-P_{0,r}).
\]
By Proposition~\ref{prop:extraction} and Theorem~\ref{thm:continuum}, this lower bound passes to
the support-visible continuum:
\[
L^{\mathrm{rep}}\ge c_*(I-P_0).
\]
Using (9), \(UHU^{-1}=L^{\mathrm{rep}}\). Conjugating the lower bound by \(U^{-1}\) gives (10),
and the spectral statement (11) follows. Since unitary equivalence preserves the nonzero spectrum
and identifies the vacuum with the constant sector, the infimum of the nonzero spectrum is the
same on both sides, giving (12).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Group-uniform form]
The proof is group-uniform. Once a compact simple \(G\) is carried by a compact-gauge
zero-obstruction OPH branch, no step uses special properties of the realized Standard Model
quotient. The inputs are compact-gauge support-visible quotient locality, exact local repair on
collars, bounded-color collar covers, refinement coherence, and support-visible continuum
extraction.
\end{remark}

\section{Exact Gap Accounting}

The proof gives more than a positive lower bound. It identifies the Hamiltonian whose gap is
being measured:
\[
H=U^{-1}L^{\mathrm{rep}}U.
\]
Therefore
\[
\operatorname{Spec}(H)\setminus\{0\}
=
\operatorname{Spec}(L^{\mathrm{rep}})\setminus\{0\}.
\]
The Yang--Mills gap is exactly the first nonzero repair eigenvalue:
\[
\Delta_{\YM}
:=
\inf\bigl(\operatorname{Spec}(H)\setminus\{0\}\bigr)
=
\inf\bigl(\operatorname{Spec}(L^{\mathrm{rep}})\setminus\{0\}\bigr)
=:
\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}.
\]
The finite-stage color argument proves \(\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}\ge c_*>0\). The exact accounting
statement is the equality \(\Delta_{\YM}=\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}\); the inequality is the positivity
proof for that same quantity.

\section{Why This Is Not the Old 2D Yang--Mills Bridge}

The OPH corpus also contains an exact edge-sector identity:
\[
Z_{\mathrm{edge}}(t)=\sum_R d_R^2e^{-tC_2(R)}=K_t(1),
\]
which is the compact-group heat kernel at the identity and matches the standard
two-dimensional Yang--Mills heat-kernel partition form. Peter--Weyl supplies the heat-kernel
identity, and Gross--Taylor gives the standard large-\(N\) worldsheet dictionary when a separate
large-\(N_{\mathrm{edge}}\) branch with remainder control is declared~\cite{peter-weyl,gross-taylor}.

That 2D result is a normalization and worldsheet-effective bridge. The mass gap above is a
different theorem. It is not a partition identity. It is a spectral lower bound for the
support-visible compact-gauge Hamiltonian, obtained by identifying Euclidean transfer with the
repair generator.

\section{Conclusion}

The OPH answer to the Yang--Mills mass gap is simple in mechanism. A non-vacuum
support-visible compact-gauge excitation is not fixed by all local repair collars. At least one
active collar must relax it. Because active collar rates have a uniform positive floor and the
collars can be organized into finitely many commuting colors, every non-vacuum state pays a
positive Euclidean repair cost. The continuum compact-gauge Hamiltonian is unitarily equivalent
to that repair generator. Therefore the Yang--Mills gap is the repair gap.

\[
\boxed{
\Delta_{\YM}=\Delta_{\mathrm{rep}}\ge c_*>0.
}
\]
