\begin{abstract}
Observer-patch holography contains a distinguished scalar repair register but
does not assign that register a propagation law. This paper states a candidate
dynamical completion: integer repair occupation and a conjugate repair phase
form a lattice rotor. The proposed action has an exact repair-current balance
law and a metric stress tensor. Its dilute homogeneous phase is pressureless,
while a cubic condensed-phase link energy gives the deep radial-acceleration
law and the baryonic Tully--Fisher scaling. Coherent material couples through
the canonical susceptibility \(\chi_\nu^{\rm can}\), producing a lawful force
only through genuine repair charge, an external repair gradient, or explicit
field-momentum exchange. The canonical pair, source normalizations, complete
constitutive law, relativistic limit, abundance, lensing, CMB, cluster, and
laboratory receipts are work in progress. The construction is a conditional
dark-sector mechanism, not a closed cosmological prediction.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Missing Dynamical Object}

A scalar channel identifies where a perturbation lives. Dynamics requires a
canonical pair. Let the fixed-cutoff OPH carrier be an oriented causal cell
complex with sites \(v\), oriented links \(e\), metric-dependent weights
\(V_v\) and \(W_e\), integer signed repair occupation \(n_v\), and conjugate
phase \(\theta_v\). The pair obeys
\begin{equation}
n_v\in\mathbb Z,
\qquad
\theta_v\in S^1.
\end{equation}
The local formulae use a real lift of \(\theta\). A global compact-phase
completion must make the exponentiated source coupling single-valued. That
quantization condition is work in progress.

Let \(S_{{\rm coh},v}^{\rm can}\) be the canonical coherent-material source
and \(\mathcal Q_{b,v}\) the baryonic repair demand. The fixed-chart
Lorentzian action is
\begin{align}
I_{\RPC}={}&I_{\rm Regge}[\ell]+I_{\rm SM}[\ell,\Psi]
\nonumber\\
&+\int\dd\tau\left[
-\sum_v V_v n_vD_\tau\theta_v
-\sum_vV_v\varepsilon(n_v)
-\sum_eW_e\mathcal K\!\left(|(d\theta)_e|\right)
\right.\nonumber\\
&\hspace{34mm}\left.
-\sum_vV_v\theta_v
\left(\mathcal Q_{b,v}+q_\star\chican
S_{{\rm coh},v}^{\rm can}\right)
\right].
\label{eq:rpc-action}
\end{align}
Here \(q_\star\) is the dimensional coherent-source calibration. The
infrared constitutive functions are
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon(n)=m_R|n|+\frac{u_R}{3}|n|^3+\cdots,
\qquad

\label{eq:constitutive}
\end{equation}
with \(m_R,u_R,\kappa_R>0\). The omitted terms must provide a positive convex
high-gradient completion.

Let \(D_\tau n_v:=V_v^{-1}\partial_\tau(V_vn_v)\), and define the lattice
divergence by
\begin{equation}

\section{Repair Charge, Current, and Stress}

Variation with respect to the phase gives
\begin{equation}
D_\tau n+\operatorname{div}_R j_R
=\mathcal Q_b+q_\star\chican S_{\rm coh}^{\rm can},
\label{eq:gauss}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
j_{R,e}=\mathcal K'\!\left(|(d\theta)_e|\right)
\frac{(d\theta)_e}{|(d\theta)_e|}.
\end{equation}
For the cubic infrared branch,
\begin{equation}
j_{R,e}=\kappa_R|(d\theta)_e|(d\theta)_e.
\end{equation}
Summation over a finite region \(\Omega\) gives the exact lattice balance law
\begin{equation}
\frac{\dd}{\dd\tau}\sum_{v\in\Omega}V_vn_v
+\sum_{e\in\partial\Omega}W_ej_{R,e}
=\sum_{v\in\Omega}V_v
\left(\mathcal Q_{b,v}+q_\star\chican
S_{{\rm coh},v}^{\rm can}\right).
\label{eq:finite-balance}
\end{equation}

Equation~\eqref{eq:finite-balance} leaves three lawful source classes. A
compact neutral source has no monopole and begins at dipole order. A source
with nonzero integrated repair charge has a noncompact field tail. A driven
source exchanges repair charge and momentum through its boundary. A compact
neutral scalar contrast cannot produce a static support force by itself.

In the static cubic branch the spatial stress is

\section{Dilute Homogeneous Phase}

Variation with respect to \(n_v\) gives
\begin{equation}
D_\tau\theta_v=-\varepsilon'(n_v).
\end{equation}
On the positive homogeneous branch,
\begin{equation}
\rho_R=m_Rn+\frac{u_R}{3}n^3,
\qquad
p_R=n\varepsilon'(n)-\varepsilon(n)=\frac{2u_R}{3}n^3.
\label{eq:eos}
\end{equation}
For \(u_Rn^2\ll m_R\), the repair medium satisfies
\begin{equation}
p_R\ll\rho_R,
\qquad
\rho_R\simeq m_Rn.
\end{equation}
With no active source, Eq.~\eqref{eq:gauss} gives repair-charge conservation.
In a homogeneous expanding chart,
\begin{equation}
\dot n+3Hn=0,
\qquad
n\propto a^{-3},
\qquad
\rho_R\propto a^{-3},
\qquad
w_R\simeq0.
\label{eq:dust}
\end{equation}
The proposed repair medium therefore contains a cold-dark-matter-like
background phase. Its abundance and perturbation transfer functions are not
fixed by Eq.~\eqref{eq:dust}.

\section{Condensed Galactic Phase}

In a static condensed region, take
\begin{equation}
\nabla\!\cdot\!\left(\kappa_R|\nabla\theta|\nabla\theta\right)
=\mathcal Q_b,
\qquad
\mathcal Q_b=\beta_b\rho_b,
\qquad
\mathbf a_R=-\beta_b\nabla\theta.
\label{eq:galaxy-system}
\end{equation}
For a spherical baryonic source of mass \(M_b\), the exterior attractive
branch satisfies
\begin{equation}
4\pi r^2\kappa_R|\theta'|\theta'=\beta_bM_b,
\end{equation}
and hence
\begin{equation}
a_R=\sqrt{\frac{\beta_b^3M_b}{4\pi\kappa_R}}\frac1r.
\end{equation}
Define
\begin{equation}
a_0=\frac{\beta_b^3}{4\pi G\kappa_R}.
\label{eq:a0}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
\boxed{a_R=\frac{\sqrt{GM_ba_0}}{r}=\sqrt{a_ba_0}},
\qquad
\boxed{v^4=GM_ba_0}.
\label{eq:deep-law}
\end{equation}
Thus the cubic link energy implies the deep radial-acceleration relation and
the baryonic Tully--Fisher scaling on the stated spherical, static,
condensed-phase premises. The complete interpolation, nonspherical dynamics,
external-field behavior, and value of \(a_0\) require the full microscopic
function \(\mathcal K\) and the source-derived constants \(\beta_b,\kappa_R\).

A single medium with a pressureless normal phase and a MOND-like condensed
response also appears in superfluid dark-matter models
\cite{berezhiani2015}. Dipolar media provide another action-based example in
which cosmological dust behavior and galactic polarization coexist
\cite{blanchet2008}. These analogies establish mathematical precedent, not an
OPH derivation.

\section{High-Gradient Branch}

The constitutive completion may have
\begin{equation}
\mathcal K(y)\sim\frac{\kappa_R}{3}y^3
\quad\text{at low gradient},
\qquad
\mathcal K(y)\sim\frac{\kappa_\infty}{p}y^p,
\quad p>3,
\quad\text{at high gradient}.
\label{eq:screening}
\end{equation}
For fixed source flux, the high-power branch makes the field gradient grow
sublinearly with source strength. This is a kinetic-screening route rather
than a system label. K-mouflage models show that nonlinear kinetic screening
can coexist with Solar-System and cosmological constraints, subject to strong
parameter bounds~\cite{barreira2015}. Equation~\eqref{eq:screening} does not
establish Solar-System viability. The Sun-plus-Galaxy boundary problem and the
relativistic coupling are work in progress.

\section{Coherent Matter and \texorpdfstring{\(\chi_\nu\)}{chi-nu}}

The coherent source in Eq.~\eqref{eq:rpc-action} is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal Q_{\rm coh}=q_\star\chican S_{\rm coh}^{\rm can}.
\label{eq:coherent-charge}
\end{equation}
The coefficient \(\chican\) is the dimensionless efficiency with which the
canonical coherent source enters the repair-charge channel. It is not a
complete force coefficient. The dimensional normalization \(q_\star\), the
ambient repair field, and the source charge are separate objects.

If an engineering chart uses
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm coh}^{\rm eng}=N_{\rm coh}S_{\rm coh}^{\rm can},
\end{equation}
then
\begin{equation}
\chi_\nu^{\rm eng}=\frac{\chican}{N_{\rm coh}}.
\end{equation}
This is a coordinate change. It fixes neither \(q_\star\) nor a device force.

A coherent source in an external repair field experiences
\begin{equation}
F_i^{\rm coh}=-q_\star\chican
\int_\Omega S_{\rm coh}^{\rm can}(x)
\,\partial_i\theta_{\rm ext}(x)\dd^3x.
\label{eq:coherent-force}
\end{equation}
Define
\begin{equation}
Q_R^{\rm dev}=q_\star\chican
\int_\Omega S_{\rm coh}^{\rm can}\dd^3x.
\end{equation}
For a slowly varying external field,
\begin{equation}
F_i^{\rm dev}\simeq-Q_R^{\rm dev}\partial_i\theta_{\rm ext}.
\label{eq:device-force}
\end{equation}
Signed repair charge permits attractive or repulsive response while
\(\varepsilon(n)=\varepsilon(|n|)\) keeps the onsite energy positive.

A local repulsive response therefore requires either
\begin{equation}
Q_R^{\rm dev}\ne0
\end{equation}
with its field tail, or nonzero field-momentum transfer in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:momentum-ledger}. A static, closed, repair-neutral device cannot
support itself through an internal top-to-bottom coherence contrast.
