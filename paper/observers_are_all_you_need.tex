\begin{abstract}
Observer-Patch Holography (OPH) is an observer-consistency framework in which
public physics is the stable result of finite observers comparing and repairing
the records visible on overlapping screen patches. Time, geometry, matter, and
history are treated as readouts of the settled observer-facing structure
produced by finite record overlap and repair.

Each observer has access only to local patch data, and overlapping patches must
agree on the records both sides can check. Public structure is therefore the
quotient normal form of bounded systems with
local state, ports or boundaries, readback, records, feedback or repair moves,
and public evidence bundles.

The paper gives the program in one pass. Overlap repair produces public records
and normal forms. A separate geometric-readout branch then adds modular screen
geometry, the three-dimensional spatial chart, Lorentz kinematics, and the
Einstein branch with its Newton limit.
Receipt-conditional compact sector reconstruction yields the Standard Model charge structure, three
colors, and three generations. The same stack carries the record interface,
Maxwell and Yang--Mills branches, action-level classical carrier modes with a separate quantum-particle gate, cosmic
capacity closure, and the hierarchy/naturality result. The review surface
separates theorem claims, branch assumptions, quantitative fixed-point
continuations, implementation evidence, and named falsifiers.
\end{abstract}

\section*{What This Paper Contributes}

OPH uses familiar mathematics without disguising it. Finite algebras, quantum channels, modular
flow, recovery maps, Jacobson's entropy-to-gravity route, Doplicher--Roberts/Tannaka
reconstruction, anomaly algebra, and fixed-point analysis all have lives before this paper. The
distinct move is to put them under one operational constraint: finite observers compare only the
records visible on overlaps, repair mismatches by declared recovery moves, and accept as public only
the normal form that survives those comparisons.

The finite observer-facing patch is the primitive object. The consensus paper proves when patch
repair has a unique quotient normal form from a fixed initial quotient state and where global
agreement fails. The substrate-neutral analysis in Ref.~\cite{observableNormalForms} proves that
the stronger comparison of endpoints from different same-boundary sources is equivalent, under
observation preservation and completeness, to injectivity of the boundary map on the consistent
quotient. It keeps that cross-source criterion separate from same-source confluence, weak
normalization, and all-schedule liveness; it does not itself establish injectivity for every
physical OPH boundary map. By itself the finite consensus theorem does not prove Lorentzian
geometry or Einstein dynamics. The compact SM/GR paper
carries the declared geometric-readout branch, controlled BW modular flow, the null-stress bridge,
bounded-interval transport, fixed-cap generalized-entropy stationarity, small-ball area variation,
remainder control, and timelike coverage into a Jacobson-type Einstein equation. The same compact
paper reconstructs compact gauge structure on a cofinal tail carrying its explicit refinement
receipt and selects the realized Standard Model quotient on the declared Minimal Admissible
Realization (MAR) branch. The particle paper carries the branch into
the pixel fixed point, the capacity-electroweak bridge, and the selected-branch hierarchy and
Higgs-naturality result. The dark-sector, Yang--Mills, string, and consciousness papers use the
same machinery with narrower support boundaries and their own gates.

The technical novelty lies in the coupling. OPH adds a finite observer-facing patch tuple with
visible interface algebras and repair instruments; a recovery-derived repair relation with a
quotient normal form; a controlled modular-geometric extraction from screen caps; a
minimal-admissibility selector that acts after compact gauge reconstruction; and two dimensionless
fixed-point closures, \(P_\star\) and \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\), with stated uniqueness tests. Broad
slogans about information, entropy, or emergence carry no proof load here. The proof load sits on
the maps, branch hypotheses, uniqueness certificates, and falsifiers named in the paper stack.
The ontological sentence below the abstract is an interpretive conclusion from that reconstruction
chain. It is not an additional axiom used to derive the physics rows.

On that geometric branch, OPH recovers the three-dimensional observer-facing spatial chart, Lorentz kinematics, the Einstein branch, the de Sitter
capacity relation for dark energy, the Standard Model quotient, exact charge quantization, three
colors, three generations, Maxwell and Yang--Mills equations on their stated branches, and the
conditional classical Maxwell, perturbative pure-Yang--Mills, and pure-Einstein carrier modes.
Quantum photon/gluon/graviton poles require the separate receipt stated in the compact and shared
derivation papers. On the quantitative side it uses two dimensionless
fixed points, \(P_\star\) and \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\), plus a selected no-\(G\) scale certificate.
Those coordinates feed the fine-structure, hierarchy, particle, and cosmology rows.

For gravity, Jacobson supplies the comparison target: Einstein gravity follows from entropy
equilibrium or a Clausius relation under local assumptions. OPH uses that target with a different
upstream and downstream structure. Upstream, the relevant regions are observer-visible screen caps
and collars; their modular flow is extracted from the controlled cap branch. The stationarity
statement is fixed-cap generalized entropy stationarity on declared MaxEnt variations, with the
collar recovery remainder, bounded-interval kernel, fixed-volume small-ball area identity, and
null-stress bridge supplied on the recovered-core geometric branch. Downstream,
timelike-direction completeness upgrades the rest-frame scalar relation to a tensor equation,
while local null data leave the metric term open. The cosmic record-capacity fixed point closes
that term. A
counterexample to any of these branch steps defeats the OPH gravity claim while leaving Jacobson's
original comparison result untouched.

The boundaries matter as much as the outputs. A theorem, a scaling branch, a fixed-point
certificate, a phenomenological continuation, and an ontology reading are different kinds of
claim. OPH fails as stated if a load-bearing theorem has a counterexample under its assumptions,
if the controlled modular branch cannot be physically instantiated, if MAR admits a smaller
realized package, if a quantitative closure is nonunique or target-leaking, or if a carrier
implementation changes the observable quotient after visible records and repair maps are held
fixed.

\section*{Canonical Observer Language}

An abstract observer patch is the algebraic object
\[
\mathsf O_i=
\left(
\mathcal A_i,\rho_i,\mathcal R_i,
\{(\mathcal I_e,\pi_{i,e})\}_{e\ni i},
\mathcal U_i,\mathrm{Chk}_i
\right).
\]
Here \(\mathcal A_i\) is the finite or regulated accessible algebra, \(\rho_i\) the accessible
state, \(\mathcal R_i\) the exact or approximate record algebra, \(\mathcal I_e\) the overlap-visible
interface algebra, \(\pi_{i,e}\) the visible restriction, \(\mathcal U_i\) the allowed update and
repair instruments, and \(\mathrm{Chk}_i\) the checkpoint data used for continuation.

A support patch, such as a cap \(P_i\subset S^2\) or a causal diamond, is a geometric chart of the
abstract patch on a geometric branch. A carrier patch is a physical or digital implementation that
realizes the same visible interface and record statistics within a declared error. Echosahedral
hardware is therefore a reference carrier architecture for a fixed-cutoff implementation surface.
The physical claim lives at the quotient of visible records, interface statistics, repair maps, and
checkpoint continuation.

The observer patch is therefore the formal access structure used by the proof. A human observer, a
horizon cap, and a hardware module can realize or chart it, but none of those examples is the
definition. Physical predictions attach to quotient data: visible interface statistics, record
readout, repair maps, and checkpoint continuation. A carrier implementation is admissible only when
those data remain invariant within its declared error model.

\begin{definition}[Semantic observer history]\label{def:semantic-observer-history}
For an observer token \(O\), the observer-readable history is a labeled causal poset
\[
\mathsf H_O=(E_O,\preceq_O,\ell_O).
\]
The elements of \(E_O\) are semantic record events, \(\preceq_O\) is physical or informational
dependence, and \(\ell_O\) is the observer-visible event label. Event identity is derived from the
canonical semantic payload, observer token, visible footprint, and semantic causal parents. It is
not derived from worker ID, repair iteration, queue position, retry counter, timestamp, or packet
latency metadata. Latency is execution gauge only when it does not alter semantic causality,
delivered data, timeout-sensitive operations, or operational clock records.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Observer registry groupoid and namespaces]\label{def:observer-registry-groupoid}
Observer identities live in a registry groupoid, not in a flat UUID table. Objects are observer
tokens
\[
\mathrm{RID}(O)=\bigl(\mathrm{kind}(O),[\mathrm{birth}(O)]_{\mathsf H},\mathrm{lineage}(O)\bigr),
\]
where \(\mathrm{kind}(O)\) lies in the disjoint namespace
\[
\{\text{patch observer},\ \text{cap observer},\ \text{future observer}\}.
\]
The birth event is a semantic event class in \(\mathsf H\), and lineage is carried by explicit
continuation, split, and merge arrows. Local registries on overlapping presentations must preserve
observer kind, birth event, lineage arrows, visible observer structure, and checkpoint cuts. The
global registry is the descent groupoid or colimit of those local registries when the cocycle and
trivial-monodromy checks pass. A split creates child tokens with parent links; it does not silently
assign one identity to multiple descendants.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Operational observer clock]\label{def:operational-observer-clock}
The modular parameter of an observer-facing cap pair is not, by itself, an arbitrary operational
clock reading. A clock claim supplies an observer-accessible instrument
\[
\Theta_O:\mathrm{Chk}_O(D)\to\operatorname{Prob}(\mathbb R)
\]
and a declared affine calibration law. For a corresponding observer \(O'\), the required finite
or exact certificate has the form
\[
\Theta_{O'}(\Phi_{O*}\rho)
\approx
(A_{a,b})_*\Theta_O(\rho),
\qquad
A_{a,b}(\tau)=a\tau+b,
\]
with an explicit residual bound from state mismatch, calibration error, and record-readout error.
Execution counters are provenance; observer record order, modular parameter, and clock
uncertainty are separate semantic or operational fields.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Observer-clock naturality gate]\label{rem:observer-clock-naturality-gate}
The present paper proves observer-first quotient and readback claims only where the cited theorem
surface supplies the required data. Scheduler-independent observer history and clock naturality
require a history-augmented quotient relation with history-coherent diamonds, registry descent,
state-preserving observer-algebra extraction, support-cap chart naturality, and the operational
clock instrument above. A public evidence bundle can falsify the claim by showing counter-dependent
semantic event keys, duplicate or namespace-colliding registry identities, broken registry
cocycles, non-isomorphic semantic history DAGs across executors, or clock residuals exceeding the
declared bound. Implementation field names alone are not the proof.
\end{remark}

This definition leaves the primitive observer size open. The axioms require finite or regulated
access at fixed cutoff and permit connected support regions with different finite or regulated
algebras. A primitive observer may therefore be a variable finite federation of screen cells. The
stronger claim that elementary carriers are isomorphic fixed-capacity cells would require a separate
branch: homogeneous UV cellulation, isomorphic local cell algebras, equivariant overlap maps,
refinement preservation of the cell type, and a proof identifying one cell or a fixed block of cells
with the primitive carrier.

\section*{Failure Tests}

OPH is wrong as stated if a load-bearing theorem admits a counterexample under its declared
assumptions, if the controlled modular/Einstein branch cannot be instantiated under physically
realizable conditions, if MAR fails to select the stated realized package on the declared
admissible class, if a quantitative closure is nonunique or target-leaking, or if a physical
implementation shows branch-essential dependence on hidden carrier presentation after visible
quotient data are held fixed. Concrete falsifiers include failure of a displayed Maxwell,
pure-Yang--Mills, or pure-Einstein quadratic kernel to have its stated transverse or TT classical
mode under all of that theorem's action/background/phase hypotheses; failure of a claimed quantum
pole after its full quantization and spectral receipt is supplied; gauge-mediated proton decay on
the realized Standard Model branch; a fourth
light matter generation in the realized low-energy branch; a smaller admissible one-Higgs Standard
Model package; nonunique \(P_\star\) or \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\) closure; a failed fixed-cap
entropy-to-Einstein tensor upgrade; or carrier-dependent observables after the visible interface is
held fixed. A failed continuation does not erase the recovered core unless that continuation is one
of the stated parents of the core claim.

\section{Introduction}

Observer-Patch Holography asks how much of physics is forced once the world is described from the
inside. No observer has access to a view from nowhere. In the idealized description there is a
shared screen net, often charted by \(S^2\), but each finite observer has access only to a local
patch \(P_O\) of that net. The observer carries records inside that patch and can compare only the
observables shared with neighboring patches. OPH takes that comparison rule literally: where
descriptions overlap, they must agree. Thus an "observer screen" is an operational access cut, not a
private physical sphere assigned to the observer.

The formalism used here is deliberately quantum-algebraic. Patches carry algebras and states,
record events use the trace/Born rule on their declared operator surface, and the gravity branch
uses generalized entropy. OPH's theory-of-everything claim is that the observed effective universe
can be recovered from this axiom set and its stated branch conditions if the framework is correct.
The reconstruction may use declared standard mathematics and branch hypotheses without treating
those ingredients as hidden empirical constants.

The computational interpretation has a sharp boundary. A conventional simulation evolves surrogate
universe states,
\[
U_t\longrightarrow U_{t+1}\longrightarrow U_{t+2}\longrightarrow\cdots .
\]
OPH uses a fixed-point computation, but the equation alone is not the definition of
simulation. In the fundamental description there is no global timeline on which the contents
of spacetime are updated. The readback-and-repair operator acts on observer-readable world
candidates and selects a stable solution
\[
\mathcal T(W)=W .
\]
The companion consensus paper~\cite{ophconsensus}, Definition
\texttt{def:oph-simulation}, requires five independent clauses: recovery-derived endogenous
update, nontrivial quotient-readable records, strict overlap-repair descent with a
schedule-independent normal form, elimination of a proper candidate basin in the selected
boundary/sector fiber, and implementation/clock closure. Its Theorem
\texttt{thm:oph-simulation} proves that the selected finite OPH packet meets those clauses
under the stated boundary and record hypotheses and then derives the fixed-point equation.
The same theorem gives an identity-map/constant-functional counterexample, so an ordinary
equilibrium is insufficient as a simulation.

The local readout of the selected normal form supplies history for observers who subjectively
experience time. Time is therefore internal record order instead of an external update
parameter. In OPH, ``simulation'' means this certified observer-facing self-consistency
structure, not a bare fixed point and not a frame renderer. The executable OPH-FPE simulator
and its finite-run receipt bundles are a separate public evidence surface for finite OPH
experiments~\cite{ophfpe}; they do not replace the theorem-level definition above.

That certified consistency demand is strong enough to organize the familiar world. The effective bulk is reconstructed
from compatible screen data. Locality comes from the way collars control recovery across patch
boundaries. Gauge freedom is the freedom to change the local description without changing any shared
observable. Records are the stable parts of a patch that can be checked by other patches. Matter is
the stable charge and excitation content that survives transport, fusion, and minimal admissible
realization.

The first major result is spacetime kinematics. Modular flow on screen caps becomes geometric,
and the compact cap-normal theorem identifies a sky direction with \(q(\Omega)=(1,\Omega)\),
represents an oriented round cap by \(n_C=(\cot\alpha,\csc\alpha\,\mathbf c)\), proves
\(\eta(n_C,q(\Omega))=0\) exactly on the cap boundary with the interior/exterior sign rule, and
gives \(n_{gC}=\Lambda_g n_C\). Future unit observer frames then form
\(H^3\simeq\mathrm{SO}^+(3,1)/\mathrm{SO}(3)\), exactly three-dimensional. A cap determines an
\(H^3\) plane/half-space, not a preferred observer point or populated bulk. On the conditional
record-population branch, quotient-visible record tokens whose calibrated modular cap responses
factor through point-local \(H^3\) sources are uniquely identifiable in exact data and stably
localized in finite noisy data. The finite localization radius is
\(R_H[(L/\alpha)\varepsilon+(2/\alpha)\sigma]\) for exact net minimization, with a positive
residual gap required before a unique finite point is reported. Generalized entropy
at fixed cap then gives the Einstein relation in the large-scale regime. The cosmological
constant is not treated as a vacuum-energy
guess. Its dimensionless capacity relation is read from the cosmic record-capacity fixed point
\(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\); with the selected scale certificate this is displayed as
\[
\Lambda_{\mathrm{CRC}}=\frac{3\pi}{G N_{\mathrm{CRC}}}.
\]
The Gibbons--Hawking entropy and the Planck-2018 late-time de Sitter benchmark give a concrete normalization~\cite{gibbonsHawking77,planck18}. With
\(R_{\mathrm{dS}}\simeq1.66\times10^{26}\,\mathrm m\) and
\(\ell_P\simeq1.616\times10^{-35}\,\mathrm m\), the bare horizon ratio is
\[
N_{\mathrm{patch}}=\left(\frac{R_{\mathrm{dS}}}{\ell_P}\right)^2\simeq1.05\times10^{122}.
\]
The entropy capacity is
\[
N_{\mathrm{scr}}=S_{\mathrm{dS}}=\frac{A_{\mathrm{dS}}}{4\ell_P^2}
=\pi N_{\mathrm{patch}}\simeq3.31\times10^{122},
\]
so \(\Lambda\ell_P^2\simeq2.85\times10^{-122}\). OPH uses \(N_{\mathrm{scr}}\) for the
Gibbons--Hawking entropy capacity and \(N_{\mathrm{patch}}\) for the bare horizon area ratio.
The input-free closure is sharper: define the OPH readback map \(F(N)\) to be the active cosmic
record capacity reconstructed by stable observers inside the universe supplied with capacity
\(N\). The cosmic record-closure capacity satisfies
\[
N_{\mathrm{CRC}}=F(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}).
\]
The fixed-point proof gives uniqueness and stability. The observed branch read-off is
\(N_{\mathrm{obs}}\simeq3.31\times10^{122}\). With the selected scale certificate, the downstream
cosmological display is \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{CRC}}=3\pi/(G N_{\mathrm{CRC}})\).

The second result is the Standard Model structure. On a cofinal tail carrying the explicit
compact-gauge refinement receipt---including coherent surjective charge pullbacks, finite block
embeddings, tensor realizations, \(3+1\)D symmetry, and compatible forgetful fibers---edge sectors reconstruct the
bosonic compact-gauge branch. On the realized low-energy branch with the explicit
one-generation/one-Higgs matter package, minimal admissible realization then selects the
compact gauge quotient
\[
\SU(3)\times\SU(2)\times\U(1)/\mathbb Z_6.
\]
The realized low-energy branch then fixes exact hypercharge, three generations, three colors,
integer charge for color singlets, and the absence of gauge-mediated proton decay. On separately
stated Maxwell, perturbative/deconfined Yang--Mills, and pure-Einstein action/phase branches, the
quadratic reductions have the corresponding classical null carrier modes. Photon, gluon, or
graviton particle claims require the additional physical-Hilbert-space and spectral-pole receipts.

The third result is quantitative and sits outside the recovered core. OPH's constants program
has two dimensionless closure coordinates, the local pixel fixed point and the global cosmic
record-closure fixed point, plus a selected no-\(G\) scale proof record. Its rule is reverse
engineering followed by fixed-point closure: observations are allowed to locate the relevant basin,
because the OPH universe is treated as a closed mathematical structure whose branch coordinates are
displayed to its internal observers; the published OPH value is then the fixed point picked
out by the declared map and uniqueness proof. The exact values \(P_\star\) and
\(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\) must be computed as closure endpoints. A nearby numerical value may mimic one
measurement, but a shifted endpoint fails the self-consistency condition for the same simulated
world. On the observed branch, the
global capacity scale is \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\simeq N_{\mathrm{scr}}\simeq3.31\times10^{122}\). The local
pixel ratio is
\[
P:=\frac{a_{\mathrm{cell}}}{\ell_\star^2},
\qquad
\gamma_\star=\frac{\ell_\star\nu_{\mathrm{Cs}}}{c},
\qquad

\]
Together, \(P_\star\) and \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\) fix
\(\Lambda_\star\ell_\star^2=3\pi/N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\) and
\(\Lambda_\star a_{\mathrm{cell}}=3\pi P_\star/N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\). They fix the dimensionless
geometry. The selected scale certificate supplies the SI scale product
\(B_\star=\Lambda_\star N_{\mathrm{CRC}}=3\pi/\ell_\star^2\).
The display \(N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\simeq3.31\times10^{122}\) is only a branch-scale approximation; the
exact gravity row uses the fixed-point capacity together with the independently supplied scale
certificate.
The \(P_\star,N_{\mathrm{CRC}}\) pair fixes a canonical geometric pixel area and a total geometric
cell count on an equal-area screen chart,
\[
K_{\mathrm{cell}}
=\frac{A_{\mathrm{screen}}}{a_{\mathrm{cell}}}
=\frac{4N_{\mathrm{CRC}}}{P_\star}
\simeq 8.12\times10^{122}.
