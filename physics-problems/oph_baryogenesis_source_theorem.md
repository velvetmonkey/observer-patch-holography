## 2. The source theorem

### Theorem 1: finite-quotient anomaly-and-current theorem

Assume the finite source packet above, and assume that the record transformation acts on the chiral fields as

\[
\psi\longmapsto e^{ir_\psi\Theta_R}\psi.

\[

\[
\boxed{
 k_{R,r}

\tag{S1}
\]

Multiplicity in color, family, or any other spectator index is included in the sum. For an \(SU(2)\) doublet, \(2T_2(\mathbf2)=1\); electroweak singlets contribute zero.

#### (c) Exact source history

Let \(p_r(\tau)\) solve

\[
\frac{d p_r}{d\tau}=p_rL_{r,T(\tau)},
\qquad p_r(\tau_i)=p_{r,i}.

\[

\tag{S2}

\[
\boxed{
\dot\Theta_{R,r}(T)=\theta_0J_{R,r}(T).

\tag{S3}

If comoving entropy is conserved, write

\[
A_s(T)=1+\frac{T}{3g_{*s}(T)}\frac{dg_{*s}}{dT},
\qquad
\dot T=-\frac{H(T)T}{A_s(T)}.

The full temperature history is therefore

\[

\boxed{

Suppose the coarse maps \(c_{sr}:Q_s\to Q_r\) preserve the CP involution and winding, intertwine the generators and clocks up to errors \(\epsilon_{sr}\), and the cofinal error sum is finite. Then \(J_{R,r}(T)\) and \(\dot\Theta_{R,r}(T)\) form a Cauchy family on every compact temperature interval. ### Proof

The path sum defining \(W_R\) is additive because \(\omega_R\) is an integral one-cocycle. Its CP transformation follows term by term from the CP-odd cocycle law, proving (a).

For (b), perform the record-phase chiral rotation in the finite-regulator fermion measure. The Jacobian is the index of the electroweak Dirac operator weighted by record charge. Summing multiplets and spectator multiplicities gives (S1). The index is integral after the physical global identifications and charge normalization are fixed.

For (c), in time \(d\tau\) the expected number of transitions \(q\to q'\) is \(p(q)L(q,q')d\tau\). Multiplication by the winding increment and summation gives (S2); multiplication by \(\theta_0\) gives (S3). For (d), the transport equation is linear and its retarded freeze-out kernel is nonnegative. The final sign is therefore the sign of the weighted source moment. A generator inversion is a coordinate change and leaves the product invariant.

For (e), pair every directed transition \((q,q')\) with its CP image \((Cq,Cq')\). Their probabilities and rates are equal while their winding increments are opposite. Every pair cancels in (S2). The master equation preserves the CP-symmetric subspace, so the cancellation holds at every time.

For (f), generator, law, winding, and clock intertwining make the one-step current defects bounded by the declared refinement defects. The telescoping estimate over the cofinal tower is Cauchy when those defects are summable. The limit is unchanged under quotient-equivalent implementations. \(\square\)
